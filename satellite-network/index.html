What this is

A network of educators building AI makerspaces, together.

The AI Tinkery is an experimental makerspace at Stanford's Graduate School of Education. The Satellite Network is how we extend the model — Playbook, Toolkit, Library, and community — to K-12 schools, universities, and educational organizations anywhere.

Every tier gets you into the same room. The paid tiers fund the staff time it takes to coach you, run professional development cohorts, and keep the Toolkit growing.

Questions

Common questions

Is the free tier actually useful, or is it a trial?

It's real. You get the Playbook, the published Toolkit/Library, both community channels, and monthly office hours. Many schools will run a great Tinkery on just the free tier. The paid tiers exist because some schools want structured coaching, custom PD, and the unpublished facilitator-grade material — and that takes our staff time.

How is "Satellite" different from just buying a curriculum?

It's a network, not a product. You get the resources, but the bigger value is the cohort PD, the private site-lead channels, and the quarterly coaching — being able to ask other practitioners "what's working for you?" and get a real answer.

What counts as a "site" in the Hub tier?

Usually one school. For universities and large community orgs, a "site" can be one campus, one program, or one extension location. If you're not sure how to count, just ask — we'd rather make it work than turn the conversation into accounting.

Do you do international?

Yes. Sliding-scale pricing for non-US schools, and we'll work in your timezone for cohort PD where possible.

What does the Stanford GSE research partnership actually mean?

If your Hub opts in, we'll include you in IRB-approved studies on AI literacy outcomes, teacher adoption, and equity. You get the data on what's working in your schools. We get the research pipeline that makes the network better for everyone.

Can I upgrade or downgrade mid-year?

Yes. Pro-rated, no penalty. The Tinkery model is about tinkering — figure out what works, change it when it doesn't.

How does billing work?

Annual invoice from Stanford. PO, credit card, and ACH all work. For Title I and sliding-scale rates, we'll send a custom invoice after the form.
